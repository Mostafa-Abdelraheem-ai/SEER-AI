# Cialdini Principles Relevant to Social Engineering

Attackers often rely on influence principles that pressure the target into bypassing normal verification.

- Authority: pretending to be an executive, bank, regulator, or IT administrator.
- Scarcity: claiming a short deadline or limited opportunity.
- Reciprocity: offering help, bonuses, or favors to trigger compliance.
- Social proof: implying everyone else has already complied.
- Commitment and consistency: asking for a small action first, then escalating.

Analysts should map message wording to these principles and treat combinations of authority plus urgency as especially high risk.
